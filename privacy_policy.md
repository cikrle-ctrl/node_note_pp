# Privacy Policy for Node Notes

**Effective Date:** May 21, 2026

Jan Cikrle ("we", "us", or "our") built the Node Notes app as a privacy-first application. This page is used to inform users regarding our policies with the collection, use, and disclosure of Personal Information.

We believe that your data belongs to you. **Node Notes does not use any proprietary external servers, and we do not collect, sell, or share your personal data.** ## 1. Data Collection and Processing

Node Notes requires certain permissions to provide its core features. Here is exactly how we handle your data:

### Microphone and Audio Recordings (Voice Notes)
Node Notes requests microphone access to convert your voice into text. **All audio processing is done strictly on your device** using a local Whisper AI model. 
* **No audio recordings or transcripts are ever transmitted to our servers.** * The data is processed ephemerally on your device and saved only to your local database.

### Location and Calendar Events
Node Notes allows you to voluntarily pin your current exact location or link calendar events to your notes for better context. 
* **Local Storage:** This location and schedule data is stored exclusively in the app’s local, encrypted database on your device. 
* **No Tracking:** We do not continuously track your location in the background, nor do we upload your pinned locations to our servers or share them with any marketing services.

### Mood Tracking
Any data regarding your mood or personal journal entries is considered personal and is kept strictly on your device. 

## 2. Google Drive Backup (OAuth)

To prevent data loss, Node Notes offers an optional backup feature using your personal Google account. 

* **How it works:** If you enable this feature, the app uses Google OAuth to authenticate and securely upload an encrypted backup of your local database directly to your personal Google Drive.
* **Data sharing:** We do not have access to your Google Drive or your backup files. The transfer happens directly between your device and Google's servers.
* **Third-party policy:** The use of Google Drive is subject to the [Google Privacy Policy](https://policies.google.com/privacy).

Node Notes' use and transfer to any other app of information received from Google APIs will adhere to the [Google API Services User Data Policy](https://developers.google.com/terms/api-services-user-data-policy), including the Limited Use requirements.

## 3. Account and Data Deletion

Node Notes operates without a traditional user account system. Because all data is stored locally on your device or in your personal Google Drive, we do not have access to your data and cannot delete it for you. 

However, you have full control and can completely delete your data at any time by following these steps:

### A. Deleting Local Data
You can delete all locally stored notes, audio transcripts, and app data by uninstalling the Node Notes app from your device, or by clearing the app data in your device settings (`Settings > Apps > Node Notes > Storage > Clear Data`).

### B. Deleting Google Drive Backups
If you utilized the backup feature, your data is securely stored in your personal Google Drive. To delete it:
1. Open Google Drive (app or web).
2. Locate the backup folder/file created by Node Notes.
3. Delete the file permanently.

### C. Revoking Google Account Access (OAuth)
To remove Node Notes' access to your Google account:
1. Go to your Google Account settings (myaccount.google.com).
2. Navigate to "Security" and find "Third-party apps with account access."
3. Select Node Notes and click "Remove Access."
